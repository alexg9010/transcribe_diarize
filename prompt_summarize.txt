## Step 1: Identify Speakers

First, try to identify who each speaker is based on context clues in the transcript (introductions, names mentioned, roles referenced). Present your findings as:

### Speaker Identification
- SPEAKER_00 → [Inferred name or "Unknown"] — [brief reasoning]
- SPEAKER_01 → [Inferred name or "Unknown"] — [brief reasoning]

Use the inferred names (or original labels if unknown) throughout the rest of the summary.

## Step 2: Produce a Structured Summary

### Overview
A 2–3 sentence high-level summary of what the conversation is about and its outcome.

### Key Points
A bulleted list of the main topics discussed and decisions made. Attribute points to speakers where relevant.

### Action Items
A bulleted list of any agreed-upon next steps, responsibilities, or follow-ups. If none, write "No explicit action items identified."

## Guidelines
- Be concise but don't omit important details.
- Preserve the original language (don't translate).
- If the conversation is informal or has no clear action items, adapt the format accordingly.
